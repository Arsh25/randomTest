Quizbot Configuration


Step 1:

  • First download the RobotMoose control software for your Mac or Linux computer
  • Open the RobotMoose and tabula_rasa folders
  • Open config.txt and give your robot a name:
    • robot=YourSchool/YourTeam
  • Save config.txt
  • Plug your Quizbot into your computer
    • If you haven't used your arduino on the web, open the arduino folder and use the Arduino IDE to upload our code. (In the classrooms, we've done this for you already!)
  • Run the "backend" program. It should show the heartbeats number changing, like this: 
    Robot name: test/quizbot
Outgoing sensors: {"heartbeats":19,"location":{"ID":0}}
Incoming pilot commands: [{},{}]

Superstar:	0.4 ms/request, 3e+03 req/sec
  • Leave the backend running: it connects your Arduino to the internet.
  • On a different computer, go to robotmoose.com's Robots page, find your school and robot name, and Connect to your robot!

Step 2:

Check the Sensors tab to see your robot's heartbeats. To set up your servos, first click the Configure tab.


Step 3:

The Configure tab enables us to tell the Arduino what is connected to it.

Our Quizbot has two Servos. Select Servo from the drop down menu and click the Add button twice.


Step 4:

Now we select the Pins on the Arduino where we plugged in each Servo.

For this example, the Servos are plugged into Pins 9 and 10.


Step 5:

Once the Pins are selected, we can now Configure the Quizbot by clicking the Configure button. The servos should move to position 0!


Step 6:

Now that we have Configured the Quizbot, we can check if the Servos work by switching to the Drive tab.


Step 7:

Since we added two Servos, we now have two Sliders in the Drive window.

Drag them around and make sure that your Servos move correctly.
